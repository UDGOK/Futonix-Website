Last updated 14 June 2026

Key takeaways

  • Futonix delivered a 6-suite ambulatory day-surgery centre in Singapore turnkey in 11 months, design through commissioning.
  • All operating theatres were built to ISO Class 7 with redundant medical gas, pressure-cascade infection control and integrated nurse-call.
  • The facility opened on schedule with a daylight-led, low-stress patient interior.

What did Futonix deliver at Helix?

Futonix designed and built a 6-suite ambulatory day-surgery centre on a single integrated contract. Design-build meant one accountable team carried the project from clinical brief through architecture, MEP engineering, construction and commissioning — with no handoff between designer and builder.

The challenge

A new operator needed clinical-grade theatres open within a single year to meet patient demand, on a constrained urban floorplate. Compliance — infection control, medical gas redundancy and theatre air quality — could not be compromised to hit the date.

The solution

Futonix modelled every system in BIM up front, clash-resolving HVAC, medical gas and structure before construction. Theatres were engineered to ISO Class 7 with pressure-cascade control; prefabricated MEP risers and a lean schedule compressed the programme without overtime risk.

The outcome

The centre opened on schedule and passed commissioning first time. The integrated model gave the client a single point of accountability and a building that was monitored and automation-ready from day one.
